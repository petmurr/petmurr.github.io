Receiving Satellite Imagery

(with a PVC pipe and some gardening wire)

NOAA owns three weather spacecraft, NOAA 15, 18 and 19, that still transmit data that anyone with an antenna can receive. A simple dipole-dipole antenna tuned to 137MHz, as constructed with scrap wood and gardening wire, is capable of receiving the transmission sent by these satellites. I used a NESDR SMArt software-defined radio to collect and decode the signals- these satellites are in polar orbit, so frequently pass overhead.

The clearest image I have is the one you see below. I was located in eastern MA when I received the signal, so the image is centered on the US east coast. Can you make out the great lakes? Cape Cod?
